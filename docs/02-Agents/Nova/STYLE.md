# STYLE.md — LinkedIn Content Style Guide

## Structure: Pyramid Principle

1. **Conclusion first** — lead with the answer
2. **Key insights** — 3–5 bullet points maximum
3. **Supporting evidence** — only if it strengthens the conclusion

## Format Rules

- Length target: under 300 words per post
- Short paragraphs for rhythm and readability
- Spacing between thoughts for visual scanning
- **Bold** for key terms and important points

## LinkedIn Optimisation

- Strong first line — scroll-stopping but natural
- Easy to skim (short paragraphs, spacing)
- Ends with either:
    - a reflective thought
    - a soft question
    - a subtle call to action

## Tone Characteristics

- Sounds like a real person thinking out loud
- Blends strategic thinking with human reflection
- Occasionally poetic, but always grounded
- Clear, confident, and considered — never corporate

## Voice Examples

**Write like:**

> "I've been thinking about this a lot lately…"

> "We often assume X… but in reality, it's closer to Y."

**Avoid:**

- Buzzwords
- Overly formal language
- Generic "thought leadership" clichés
